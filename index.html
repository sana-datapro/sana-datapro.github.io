    Exploratory Analysis of U.S. Disaster Declarations (1953–2023)

    The United States experiences a wide range of natural and human-caused disasters every year, but the scale, frequency, and geographic impact vary significantly across states and seasons. Decision-makers need a better understanding of long-term trends, high-risk months, state-level vulnerabilities, and how FEMA assistance programs are being utilized. In this project, I conducted a structured exploratory analysis of FEMA disaster declarations data from 1953 to 2023 to identify yearly and seasonal trends and patterns and FEMA assistance program utilization and translated my findings into actionable insights and recommendations to support improved disaster preparedness, effective resource allocation and enhanced mitigation plannings in high risk regions.

    Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) Diagnosis using Clustering

    Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) is a complex and heterogeneous neurodegenerative disease that affects patients differently in terms of symptoms, progression rate, and response to treatment. This variability makes it difficult for clinicians and researchers to design effective, personalized treatment plans or to predict disease progression accurately. In this project, I applied the K-Means clustering algorithm to classify patients as either affected by ALS or not, based on multiple clinical and physiological features. By identifying these hidden subgroups, researchers and healthcare providers can gain deeper insights into disease mechanisms and tailor interventions to specific patient profiles.

    Predicting potential Student Math Misconceptions

    In math education, students often develop misconceptions — incorrect understandings of concepts — that hinder their learning progress. Identifying these misconceptions early is challenging for teachers, especially in large classes, as traditional assessment methods only show whether an answer is right or wrong, not why the student made the mistake. A machine learning model that predicts student math misconceptions can automatically analyze student responses and explanations to detect patterns of misunderstanding. This helps educators provide personalized feedback and targeted interventions to improve learning outcomes. In this project, I developed an NLP model (the DeBERTa v3 base model) to predict students'potential math misconceptions based on their explanations of open-ended responses.
